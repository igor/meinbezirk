# Contributing to MeinBezirk

This toolkit was built for Berlin. But the problem is universal. Local governments everywhere make decisions that affect citizens — and citizens everywhere struggle to find out what those decisions are.

The toolkit and its reference implementation are in German. The methodology is transferable to any city in any country.

## Contributing a new city (Germany)

Adapting MeinBezirk for another German city:

1. Fork this repo.
2. Follow the methodology in [02-daten-finden](02-daten-finden/) to find your city's data sources.
3. Adapt the filters in [03-relevanz-gestalten](03-relevanz-gestalten/) to your community.
4. Build a prototype following the principles in [04-umsetzen](04-umsetzen/).
5. Share your case study back as a pull request.

Especially valuable: documenting which Ratsinformationssystem your city uses and how to access its data. Every city handles this differently. That documentation helps everyone.

## Für deutsche Civic-Tech-Communities

Du bist Teil eines [Code for Germany](https://codefor.de) Labs oder einer lokalen Civic-Tech-Gruppe? Dann bist du die Zielgruppe dieses Toolkits. Die OK Labs kennen die Datenlandschaft ihrer Stadt, haben Kontakte in die Verwaltung und wissen, welche Ratsinformationssysteme lokal im Einsatz sind.

Was besonders hilft: Dokumentiere, wie die Datenrecherche in deiner Stadt funktioniert hat. Welches System wird genutzt? Wo liegen die Hürden? Was fehlt? Diese Erfahrungsberichte machen das Toolkit für die nächste Stadt brauchbarer.

Kontakt zu den Labs: [codefor.de](https://codefor.de). Die [Open Knowledge Foundation Deutschland](https://okfn.de) koordiniert das Netzwerk.

## Contributing from another country

The methodology is transferable. The data landscape will be different.

- Start with [01-verstehen](01-verstehen/). The problem statement is universal.
- Document how your country's local government publishes decisions.
- Map the equivalent of Ratsinformationssysteme in your context. What systems exist? Who maintains them? What data is public?
- Consider translating the toolkit into your language.

We welcome adaptations for any country.

## What makes a good contribution

- Case studies from real cities. Not hypothetical scenarios.
- New data source patterns. Systems not yet documented.
- New relevance dimensions. Filter axes not yet considered.
- Translations of the toolkit.
- Corrections and improvements to existing content.
- Bug reports and suggestions via issues.

## How to contribute

1. Fork the repo.
2. Create a branch.
3. Make your changes.
4. Open a pull request.

No special tooling required. Content is Markdown.

One contribution per pull request. Write clearly. Short sentences. Plain language.
